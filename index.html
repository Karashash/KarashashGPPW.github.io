Project work

The influence of COVID-19 on education in Kazakhstan

How to keep the academic productivity of students during the pandemic?

Abstract

The coronavirus has led us to connect to distance learning, which has changed a lot in the lives of students, creating non-traditional teaching methods and lesson formats. Consequently, these changes affected the students' academic performance and their approach to learning. For the reasons listed above, I took up the study of this problem. For the study, I took students from the city of Aktobe, Kazakhstan, so I had the opportunity to analyze the performance of students in this area. For the investigation, I took both quantitative and qualitative research methods. The interview was in the role qualitative and the survey quantitative. Furthermore, I used data from research papers with a similar purpose to mine. As a result of the data collection, I analyzed how the academic performance of students has changed and exactly what difficulties they have encountered during online learning. As a recommendation for solving the problem, about 96% of respondents suggested switching back to offline training. Summing up my research work, I can say that most students have problems with mastering the topic of lessons. The reason for this is little interaction with the teacher and students. And the participants in the data collection said that for many, traditional training is more convenient and only this can save their state in school.

Key words: academic performance, COVID-19, negative, positive, impact.
